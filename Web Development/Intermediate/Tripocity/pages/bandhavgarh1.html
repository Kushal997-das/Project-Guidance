Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh in Madhya Pradesh is a place of legendary significance. The ancient Bandhavgarh Fort is of great importance as it is believed to have been gifted by Lord Rama to his younger brother Lakshmana to keep a watch on Lanka. Therefore this fort is named Bandhavgarh as "Bandhav" means brother and "Garh" means fort Bandhavgarh dates back to 2000 years ago and you will find several man made caves with inscriptions and rock paintings. Once a hunting ground of maharajas, Bandhavgarh is now Bandhavgarh National Park and home to the royal Bengal tiger, 37 other species of mammals, 250 species of birds, 80 species of butterflies. Bandhavgarh National Park, Madhya Pradesh is the place to visit if you want to spot the big cat. India's top dwelling for tigers, it has the highest density of Royal Bengal tigers in the world. All the white tigers of the world trace their roots to Bandhavgarh National Park. Bandhavgarh National Park, Madhya Pradesh is the first in India to launch 'The Hot Air Balloon Safari' for tourists. You can now explore the unseen gems of Bandhavgarh National Park at its resplendent best. An aerial view of the restricted buffer zone and core zone will be an exclusive experience for the safari lovers.

Divine Amarkantak (188 KM)

Amarkantak is a well frequented Hindu pilgrimage center that is popularly known as "Teerthraj" - "the king of pilgrimages". It is the meeting point of the Vindhya and the Satpura Ranges, with the Maikal Hills being the fulcrum. The forests surrounding Amarkantak have a rich variety of plants with medicinal properties, which make it extremely significant from an ecological point o f view.

How to Reach Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh is connected via road from all the major places in the country. The nearest railhead is at Umaria which is about 35 km from the park's entrance. You can take a cab from there. The nearest airport is at Khajuraho (270 km), and it takes a 7-8 hour drive from Khajuraho to reach here..

Jabalpur is the nearest airport to Bandhavgarh and is about 200 km which takes around 4:30 hrs from there. Air India and Spice Jet operates from Delhi-Jabalpur and Mumbai-Jabalpur flight. The most convenient way is to hire a taxi from the airport to travel to Bandhavgarh.

Nearest Airport: Jabalpur Airport (JLR) - 114 kms from Bandhavgarh National Park

No bus service are offered from Jabalpur to Bandhavgarh. However, the tiger reserve is easily accessible by road through cars.

The nearest railway station is Umaria which is very well connected to cities like Delhi, Jabalpur, and Agra. A taxi can be hired from Umaria up to Bandhavgarh.

Jeep Safari and Elephant Safaris are available in the park.

What is the best time to visit Bandhavgarh National Park?

The park opens its gate for the visitors from October-mid to June end and the best time to visit Bandhavgarh National Park is from November to February. The peak season also lasts from November to February as the climatic conditions are excellent.

More about Best Time to Travel to Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park in Summer (February-June)

Heat waves start to strike in April as summer begins to set in. Since the vegetation all dries up, the sighting of animals becomes very easy, as they collect around water bodies to get relief from the dry, intentse heat. This is the best time for tiger safari, with the highest probability of sighting them resting or having water. 

However, be prepared to brave the intense heat - temperatures are frequently over 40 degrees during the safari, and while the trees give shade from the sun, it still can be really hot. Wear light cotton cloth, use caps or hats, wear sunglasses and don't forget to carry bottles of water with you during the safari. 

Try to do the safari in early morning instead of afternoon/evening, since the temperatures are relatively moderate in the morning.

Bandhavgarh National Park in Monsoon (July - September)

Bandhavgarh National Park is closed during the monsoon months of July to September, so be alerted to not plan your trip during these months.

Bandhavgarh National Park in Winter (October-February)

These are the best months for visiting Bandhavgarh National Park. 
For one, the weather is pleasant - around 25 degrees - which is quite comfortable. Also, it is during these winter months that the animals mate - if one is lucky, they can see tigers mating in the wilderness. 
If you are not only interested in the tigers, you will be able to spot a variety of birds as well. And the flora of the forest is in peak season during these months - spending time deep inside the lush green forest is an experience in itself.

Be advised, however, that the months of November and December are peak tourist seasons, because of which most of the hotels are sold out in advance, and have their peak prices during this season.
