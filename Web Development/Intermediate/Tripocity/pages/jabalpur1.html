Jabalpur Dhuadhandhar waterfall

It is a mesmerising experience to witness the marble rocks adjoining the graceful Narmada River in Bhedaghat, Jabalpur. These soaring white rocks, are known to magically change shapes as you sail along the river. Treat yourself with a boat ride on a moonlit night at the marble rocks when it shines like silver. The boatman regales you with a witty description based on his imagination and explains about vicinity in the most creative way. The tranquil Narmada flows through high marble rocks and at a short distance, sited the remarkable spectacle of nature; Dhuandhar falls. The waterfall has been named so because of the smoky appearance of the water falling off the cliff with utmost force. The best time to visit the falls is during the monsoon when Dhuandhar lives up to its name and creates a mass of mist. The waterfall flows with such power that its roar is heard from a far distance. While these marvels of nature flaunt their beauty, the magnificent 10th-century Chaunsath Yogini Temple in the same town of Bhedaghat dedicated to the goddess Durga will take you back in time with its unmatched artistry. The picturesque sites of Bhedaghat town which include an incredible spectacle of nature and heritage will definitely put a smile on your face, bringing alive the true traveller in you. If these enchanting tourist attractions don't entice you to visit the Jabalpur district then what will!

What is the best time to visit Jabalpur?

Jabalpur can be visited throughout the year, though the best time to visit Jabalpur is from September to March. The climate here is humid subtropical, typical to the south-central region over the Deccan. So the best time to visit Jabalpur is around winter when the temperatures are low, and the climate is very much pleasant. From September to February, Jabalpur sees quite a rush of tourists crowding its major attractions, Marble Rocks, Madan Mahal Fort, Rani Durgavati Museum and more. Dhuandhar Falls and Chausatyogini Temples are also some interesting places for sightseeing. Summers must be avoided as the temperature can reach as high as 45 degree Celsius.

More about Best Time to Travel to Bhedaghat


Bhedaghat in Summer (March - June)

Summers in Bhedaghat begins from April and continues till June. With temperatures rising to 34-degree Celsius, this place isn't quite ideal to visit during summers. Sightseeing becomes a challenging task as it's too hot to roam around in the sun. Boating and other adventure activities are also shut down. However, it's an excellent time to visit for budget travellers who don't mind a little heat.


Bhedaghat in Monsoon (July - September)

The monsoon season in Bhedaghat begins from July and continues till September. The weather during this time of the year is pleasing but this season is not a great time to plan your trip to Bhedaghat. All adventure activities are shut during this time from boat rides around Marble Rocks to visiting the Dhuandhar Waterfalls. The heavy rains might hinder your travel plans, however, if you wish to stay indoors and relish the enchanting beauty of Bhedaghat during monsoons you can pay a visit to this place as there might be considerable discounts in hotel tariffs.


Bhedaghat in Winter (October - February)

The winters in Madhya Pradesh is not extreme which makes Bhedaghat an ideal destination for winter break. The weather is lovely during this time and gives you an unforgettable experience. The average temperature is between 10 - 20 degrees Celcius. The mild winter season of Bhedaghat is ideal to pay a visit to the various spellbinding places that it has to offer.

This is the perfect time to explore the fascinating and picturesque Marble Rocks. Furthermore, you can also indulge in boating during the winter season to witness the beauties of this place to the fullest. A boating ride there will be a magical experience.

The winter season is also a great time of the year to pay of visit to the pristine Chausath Yogini Temple. This temple looks remarkably serene covered in mist. The winter season is perfect to roam around this place witnessing the rich culture of this place. You can even take a trip to the market and shop various colourful handcrafted items as souvenirs or gifts to your loved ones. Keeping all these factors in mind, the winter season is the best time of the year to take a trip to Bhedaghat.
