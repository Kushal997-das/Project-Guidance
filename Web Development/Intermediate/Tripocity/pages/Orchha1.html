Orchha Nature Reserve

Orchha (or Urchha) is a town in Niwari district of Madhya Pradesh state, India.The historic town of Orchha, nestled on the banks of river Betwa, was founded in the 16th century by the Bundela Rajput Chief, Rudra Pratap. The Betwa River, on whose banks Orchha lies, and the forests around it attract tourist to this place. The architectural splendor of the monuments in Orchha reflects the glory of its rulers. Here, the river Betwa splits into seven channels, also called the Satdhara. Legend goes that this is in honour of the seven erstwhile Chiefs of Orchha.
The ancient town seems frozen in time, with its many monuments continuing to retain their original grandeur even to this day. Here you will find some of the most fascinating temples and palaces that will help you realise a childhood fantasy - travsitelling back in time!

How to Reach Orchha

Orchha has its own railway station, but the nearest major railway station is at Jhansi (15 km). Travelling from Jhansi to Orchha can be done in two way- you can either take an auto-rickshaw or go for a more expensive but comfortable cab. Orchha is also pretty close to Khajuraho and a lot of tourists typically visit Orchha from Khajuraho. They can reach here by taking a train to Orchha or to Jhansi and then a cab or auto rickshaw after that.

There is no direct flight connectivity to Orchha. The nearest airport is in Gwalior, some 140 away from the same.

What is the best time to visit Orchha

October to March is the best time to visit Orchha. However, you can also visit during the monsoons as rains are mostly average here. The town is exuberant during the festive season of Dusshera and in winters when the weather is pleasant making it an ideal time to explore the historical and architectural glory of Orchha. Summers invite extreme temperature and are usually avoided by travellers.

More about Best Time to Travel to Orchha

Orchha in Summer (March - June)

This is the least advisable time for a visit to this destination. With temperatures ranging from 23 to 47 degrees Celsius, the months of March to June are the least preferred for a visit to Orchha. The days are hot and humid, to the extent that's unbearable, especially if you're visiting in May & June. If you choose to visit Orchha during these months, ensure to be well prepared with light cotton clothes and plenty of water for hydration!

Orchha in Monsoon (July - September)

The months from July to September are great respite from the intense heat of Summer. However, heavy rainfall and unpredictable showers during this time could hamper your travel plans. On the brighter side, the monsoon rejuvenates the natural beauty of this hilly region. This is also an ideal time for budget travellers looking for off-season discounts and nature photographers who would fall in love with it's beauty.

Orchha in Winter (October - February)

Starting in October and lasting till February, the winter time is a favourite among tourists! These months have pleasant weather, cold nights and windy days with temperatures ranging from 10 to 35 degrees Celsius. The weather at this time of the year is perfect for sightseeing and exploring this charming tourist destination. People can also indulge in outdoor activities such as river-rafting, boating and shopping. Be sure to plan your trip well in advance for the best prices on accommodations as it is the peak season in Orchha.
